Questions

  1. How is Wanda a feminist critique of either CHC’s realism; Neo-realism’s sentimentalism; melodrama’s feminine subject? What is its socio-ideological argument about gender? Class? And/or film itself?
  2. Wanda’s only two moments of personal resistance are 1) puking in refusal of Mr. Dennis’ plan and 2) when the solider rapes her. Why? What are the cultural implications of her inert character arc on film’s spectator identification?
  3. Discuss the ideological meaning of Mr. Dennis’ plan that involves an invasion of the family /domestic space?
  4. Fjoralba Mairaka argues that Wanda is an anti-hero, anti-genre road movie. Do you agree? If so, how does its anti-heroism and deconstructive genre differ ideologically from any of the films we’re seen before? Select a specific sequence as proof.
  5. Contrast the political effect of the film’s use of contemporary time vs historical time with previous film. How does/What is Wanda comment on the use of contemporary history/ the present tense as setting by Neo-realism or the French New Wave?
  6. Analyze the last scene and its freeze frame. Does it function as conclusion? Resolution? Commentary?
