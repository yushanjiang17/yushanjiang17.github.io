Questions

  1. Why does the film make a clear distinction between Umberto, as an elderly pensioner and the many beggars he encounters? What is its soci0-political curiosity?
  2. Many original critics denounce De Sica’s use of the dog; they felt that it tipped the political work of the film into sentimentalism. Current critics disagree. Does Filke function as a sentimentalizing agent that obscures the film’s political work or does FIlke contribute to the film’s political work? In either case, if so prove through a specific scene.
  3. Andre Bazin celebrates the use of “’montage of attraction’{ in which a series of shots/scenes]create a sense of meaning NOT objectively contained in the shots/scenes themselves but derived exclusively from their juxtaposition.” Where does De Sica utilize this technique and for what “copulative” meaning? Is this parallel to Rossellini’s concept of Neo-realism as ‘choral”?
