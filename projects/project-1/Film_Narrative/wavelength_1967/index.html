Questions

  1. P. Adams Sitney labeled this film the best example of the "structural film," that is a film that employs "fixed camera position, the flicker effect, loop printing, re-photography off the screen. How do any of these film gestures create story?
  2. Snow understands this film as a fiction film, not a sensory film. What is its narrative? Is there a genre to which it refers? How do the film’s sensory gestures determine the narrative?
  3. Critics see the narrative effect of this film as disrupting the relationships between illusion and fact, space and time, and subject and object. If so, where is the spectator position? Who is created as the spectator?
  4. How does the sound affect the story? The creation of the spectator position?
  5. How do the visuals affect the story? The creation of the spectator position?
  6. How does the editing affect the story? Or is it affecting the narrative?
  7. n which of Wm. Earle’s categories of realism would you place the film? Why?
