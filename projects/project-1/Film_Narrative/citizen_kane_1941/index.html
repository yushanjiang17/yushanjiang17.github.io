Citizen Kane 1941 119 min.

  • Director: Orson Welles
  • Screenplay: Herman J. Mankiewicz, Orson Wells
  • Producer: Orson Welles
  • Cinematography: Robert Wise
  • Editor: Kenneth Anger
  • Music: Bernard Herman

Cast.

  • Joseph Cotten as Jedediah Leland, Kane's best friend and a reporter for The Inquirer. Cotten also appears (hidden in darkness) in the News on the March screening-room
  • Dorothy Comingore as Susan Alexander Kane, Kane's mistress and second wife.
  • Agnes Moorehead as Mary Kane, Kane's mother.
  • Ruth Warrick as Emily Monroe Norton Kane, Kane's first wife.
  • Ray Collins as Jim W. Gettys, Kane's political rival for the post of Governor of New York.
  • Erskine Sanford as Herbert Carter, editor of The Inquirer. Sanford also appears (hidden in darkness) in the News on the March screening-room
  • Everett Sloane as Mr. Bernstein, Kane's friend and employee at The Inquirer.
  • William Alland as Jerry Thompson, a reporter for News on the March. Alland also voices the narrator of the News on the March newsreel
  • Paul Stewart as Raymond, Kane's butler.
  • George Coulouris as Walter Parks Thatcher, a banker who becomes Kane's legal guardian
  • Fortunio Bonanova as Signor Matiste, vocal coach of Susan Alexander Kane
  • Gus Schilling as John, headwaiter at the El Rancho nightclub. Schilling also appears (hidden in darkness) in the News on the March screening-room
  • Philip Van Zandt as Mr. Rawlston, News on the March open at the producer
  • Georgia Backus as Bertha Anderson, attendant at the library of Walter Parks Thatcher
  • Harry Shannon as Jim Kane, Kane's father
  • Sonny Bupp as Charles Foster Kane III, Kane's son
  • Buddy Swan as Charles Foster Kane, age eight
  • Orson Welles as Charles Foster Kane, a wealthy newspaper publisher
  • Charles Bennett as entertainer at the head of the chorus line in the Inquirer party sequence
  • Gregg Toland as an interviewer depicted in part of the News on the March newsreel
  • Actor Alan Ladd, still unknown at that time as a reporter smoking a pipe at the end of the film.

Questions

  1. What is the film’s story? What is its narrative? How does the narrative ‘deform/decenter,’ or ‘reproduce/support’ the traditional CHC story?
  2. How does the film use, revise, or deform CHC’s double causal plot? To what ideological end? Who are the ‘lovers’ of that plot?
  3. Analyze the implicit theme of the film’s soundscape.
  4. Consider the implicit effect of the film’s editing? Define it a la Eisenstein?
  5. Consider this film in relation to Wm. Earle’s discussion on ‘realism’. What is the ‘real’ in this film?
  6. This film is famous for mainstreaming the ‘deep focus’ shot. ‘Deep focus’ cinematography is a camera angle that allows the cinematographer to keep everything in the shot in perspective (clear) without favoring foreground, mid-ground, or background. What is the narrative and storied effect of this film’s use of ‘deep focus’? What is the ideological effect of this film’s use of ‘deep focus’? Give an example.
  7. Colin MacCabe argues that a film’s realism is secured by the unfolding of knowledge, e.g., stating somethings while eliding others; what is the knowledge narrative of Citizen Kane? What ‘real’ does it produce? Does it make the spectator feel secure? In control?
  8. Given the structure of the narrative, what is spectator position does the film create? What is their POV?
  9. What is the explicit, implicit, and/or symptomatic discussion of the film’s title?
