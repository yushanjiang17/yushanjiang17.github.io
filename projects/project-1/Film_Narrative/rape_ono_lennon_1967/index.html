  • Cinematography: Nic Knowland

Questions

  1. What is the effect on the spectator position of the non-subtitled, untranslated dialogue of the main character?
  2. What is the film’s narrative on technology? Define the arc of the narrative?
  3. Does the spectator position change vis a vis the camera’s position, i.e., tracking shot, excessive close-up, plan américan, etc.? If so, how so and why?
  4. Does the change in setting from outdoor to indoor, from alone to unseen companions, effect the spectator position?
  5. Does the documentary effect depend on, transform, decenter, or resist the pleasure of fictional horror /stalker genre that Alfred Hitchcock made part of the public conversation about gender, the gaze, and the camera?
  6. Identify the subject of the film. Analyze a scene to support your choice.
  7. With whom does the spectator identify? Why? Is it a stable identification? If so, how so and to what ideological end? If not, when & why does it waver, and so, to what ideological end.
  8. What effect does the length of the film have on the audience? Do any narrative expectations arise, a la Wavelength? If so, what are they and why? If not, why not?
  9. What is the difference between the camera effect in this film in comparison to Wavelength? Scorpio Rising?
  10. Is the meta-camera effect similar or dissimilar to Breathless? If so, how and to what effect? If not, why and to what effect?
  11. The Fluxus group believed that “the world itself was a work of art” to challenge the limits of traditional aesthetic boundaries between representation and reality. Does Rape’s medium, i.e., documentary film, adhere to those concepts?
  12. As a FILM—it is edited and there are visual and aural acknowledgements of “take” changes— does Rape indict, make complicit, free, or neutralize the responsibility of the spectator? Analyze a sequence to illustrate your position.
