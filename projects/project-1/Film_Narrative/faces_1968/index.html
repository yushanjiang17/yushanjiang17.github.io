Questions

  1. It is argued that Cassavetes refuses to construct a subject position in the film and so does not determine a spectator position. Is this demonstrated in Faces? If so, how does an audience view this film? From where? If not, who is the subject? Proof in an analysis of a film sequence?
  2. Cassavetes films with a camera ‘in the midst’ of the actors, eschewing establishing shots, linear cause and effect, even a controlling POV. Does the spectator become the camera? If so, how and to what cultural effect? If not, why not and to what cultural effect?
  3. Are we/audience in the film as part of the action? Watching the film as voyeurs, the CHC position? Watching the film as pure spectacle, sort of rubber-necking a disaster? What is the arc of the knowledge narrative vis a vis any of these positions?
  4. Who is/are the protagonist(s) in the film? Are there any? How does the film establish them?
  5. How does this ‘noncontemplative” film form compare in to the Sirkian family melodrama in terms of social critique?
  6. How does this ‘noncontemplative” film form compare to Neo-realism’s use of a documentary gaze in fictional film? Similarities and Dissimilarities of form and effect?
